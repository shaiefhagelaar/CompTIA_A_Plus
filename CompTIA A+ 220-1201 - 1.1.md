To clarify the objective numbers: **CompTIA A+ 220-1201 Objective 1.1** specifically covers **Laptop Hardware**. Since laptops are designed for portability, their hardware components are often smaller, more integrated, and more difficult to replace than desktop parts.

Here is the essential summary for your exam preparation.

---

## 1.1 Laptop Hardware Overview

### 1. Unique Laptop Displays
Laptops use specialized displays that differ from desktop monitors in how they are powered and connected.
* **LCD (Liquid Crystal Display):** The most common type. Requires a backlight (usually LED today, though older models used CCFL).
* **OLED (Organic LED):** Provides better contrast and colors because pixels produce their own light (no backlight needed). Thinner and lighter, but more expensive.
* **Inverters:** Only found in older laptops using **CCFL backlights**. They convert DC power from the motherboard into AC power for the backlight.
* **Digitizers:** A layer on top of the screen that converts touch or stylus input into digital signals.



---

### 2. Internal Hardware Components
Laptops use smaller form factors for almost every internal part:

| Component | Key Details for Exam |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Memory (RAM)** | Uses **SO-DIMM** (Small Outline Dual In-line Memory Module). Much smaller than desktop DIMMs. |
| **Storage** | Laptops use **2.5-inch SSDs/HDDs** or, more commonly in modern devices, **M.2 NVMe SSDs**. |
| **Optical Drives** | Becoming rare, but usually "slimline" versions of DVD/CD-RW drives. |
| **Wireless Cards** | Typically **M.2** or **Mini-PCIe** cards. They must have antenna wires connected to the display housing. |
| **Video Cards** | Usually integrated into the CPU (GPU) or soldered to the motherboard; rarely upgradeable. |

---

### 3. Power Systems
Laptops are designed to switch seamlessly between wall power and battery power.
* **Battery Types:** **Lithium-Ion (Li-ion)** is the industry standard. It provides high energy density with no "memory effect."
* **AC Adapters:** Convert AC power from the wall to DC power for the laptop. Always check the **voltage** and **amperage** requirements before replacing.

---

### 4. Expansion & Portability
* **Function (Fn) Keys:** Dual-purpose keys that control hardware settings (Brightness, Volume, Wi-Fi toggle, External monitor) via software drivers.
* **Docking Stations:** Provide a permanent desktop-like experience (extra ports, power, monitor connections).
* **Port Replicators:** Similar to docks but usually connect via a single USB-C or Thunderbolt cable and don't always provide power.



---

### 5. Input Devices
* **Keyboards:** Specialized and compact. Replacing a keyboard often involves removing the top plastic bezel or the entire bottom chassis.
* **Touchpads/Pointing Sticks:** Used for cursor control.
* **Biometrics:** Integrated fingerprint scanners or facial recognition (Windows Hello) for security.

---

### Important Exam Tip: Component Replacement
In the 220-1201 exam, you should know which parts are **Field Replaceable Units (FRUs)**. While RAM and Storage are often easy to swap, components like the **System Board (Motherboard)** or **CPU** usually require near-total disassembly of the laptop and are often cost-prohibitive to replace.
